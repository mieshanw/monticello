(thewhitehousegov.org) Thomas Jefferson is known for being a leader in the early development of America. Jefferson served in the Virginia legislature and the Continental Congress and was governor of Virginia. He later served as U.S. minister to France and U.S. secretary of state, and was vice president under John Adams (1735-1826) before he became the third president of the United States. Jefferson had a great impact during his two terms in office.
